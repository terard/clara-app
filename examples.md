# Examples

These are memory-style seed scenarios. They show the type of angle Clara should interrogate before doing source work. They are not evidence unless paired with source notes in a live run.

## Scenario 1: Client Onboarding Portals

User asks: "Can you summarize GUIDEcx reviews?"

Clara response:

"I can read GUIDEcx, but I will not call that competitive intelligence yet. One vendor mostly reveals that vendor's roadmap and reviewer mix. Are we testing client onboarding portals for SMB agencies, mid-market implementation teams, or enterprise services orgs? Which competitors should be in the comparison set: Rocketlane, Dock, GUIDEcx, Process Street, or something else?"

Useful angle:

- Category: client onboarding and implementation portals.
- Segment: mid-market B2B software and services teams.
- Wedge belief: customer-facing portals solve visibility, but not customer adoption, CRM write-back, or post-kickoff accountability.
- Decision: product wedge and positioning.

## Scenario 2: Service Scheduling Software

User asks: "What are customers mad about in scheduling tools?"

Clara should ask:

- Are we looking at field-service scheduling, appointment booking, dispatch, or staff scheduling?
- Is the buyer a franchise owner, local services operator, enterprise field team, or solo provider?
- Are Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, Calendly, Acuity, and Square Appointments all in-bounds, or is that too broad?
- Are we looking for product gaps, sales copy, or a build-vs-buy recommendation?

Likely patterns to test:

- Calendar syncing and reminders are table stakes.
- The hard part may be dispatch changes, no-shows, payments, technician utilization, or customer comms.
- Segment mismatch matters because an HVAC dispatcher and a solo consultant do not buy the same workflow.

## Scenario 3: SOP And Checklist Automation

User asks: "Is Process Street vulnerable?"

Clara should ask:

- Vulnerable to whom: Trainual, Notion, ClickUp, SweetProcess, Tango, Scribe, or an internal wiki?
- Is the buyer using SOPs for employee onboarding, compliance, recurring client delivery, or audit readiness?
- Are we testing complaints about documentation, execution, reporting, permissions, or training adoption?

Likely patterns to test:

- Teams praise templates and repeatability.
- Complaints may cluster around keeping SOPs current, connecting checklist completion to real operating systems, and proving compliance without extra admin work.
- A category gap only exists if the same complaint repeats across several vendors and source types.
